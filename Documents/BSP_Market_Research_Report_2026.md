**SECTION 1 - MARKET OVERVIEW**

## 1.1 Market Definition

**IN SCOPE**

- Cloud/SaaS project management platforms for general contractors, specialty contractors, and remodelers
- Estimating, job costing, scheduling, change orders, client communication tools
- Field operations apps (daily logs, time tracking, inspections)
- CRM and lead management purpose-built for construction
- Integrated financial management for construction (invoicing, budgets, progress billing)

**OUT OF SCOPE**

- BIM/CAD software (Autodesk Revit, ArchiCAD) - design tools, not management platforms
- General-purpose PM tools (Monday.com, Asana, Trello) - horizontal, not vertical
- Pure accounting software (QuickBooks, Sage Intacct) - financial back-office only
- Enterprise ERP (Oracle Construction Cloud, Viewpoint Vista) - target \$50M+ revenue firms
- Field service management platforms (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro) - not construction PM
- Safety/compliance-only software and equipment management software

## 1.5 Market Lifecycle Stage

**Assessment: GROWING - transitioning from Early Growth toward Mid-Growth**

**Rationale:**

- Large enterprise segment (Procore, Oracle, Autodesk) are entering late growth / early maturity with consolidation underway
- SME/small contractor segment is still in early-to-mid growth: low penetration, high churn, many green-field opportunities

## 4.4 Unmet Needs & Pain Points (The Whitespace)

- True SME Pricing Without Feature Gating - The \$99-\$299/month full-featured tier is largely absent from the market.
- AI-Assisted Estimating for Small Contractors - Only 1.5% of contractors have AI use across multiple processes. No SME-tier tool has made AI estimating genuinely accessible to 1-10 person shops.
- Integrated CRM + Lead Management - Construction CRM is broadly weak across all SME tools. CRM features consistently highlighted as insufficient in G2/Capterra reviews.
- Data Portability Guarantee - Buildertrend data retrieval is notoriously difficult. Contractors fear lock-in; a credible open-export policy is a genuine differentiator.
- Simplified Subcontractor Collaboration - A lightweight sub portal (no login required, link-based) is a meaningful and unserved gap.
- Renovation-Specific Workflow - No tool is purpose-built for renovation phases: existing conditions documentation, permit sequencing, homeowner displacement planning, phased billing.
- QuickBooks Online Integration Done Right - Nearly every tool claims it; nearly every user complains it is broken. A deep, reliable integration is a true and defensible differentiator.

## 4.5 Switching Costs and Loyalty Dynamics

- Switching costs are moderate, not high - data export is possible from most platforms, though painful
- CoConstruct abandonment (since 2021) has created a large pool of migration-ready customers actively looking for alternatives
- Buildertrend's price increases over 2023-2025 have accelerated churn among small operators
- Retention is high once embedded: contractors build custom templates, train staff, and store years of project data - churn happens at renewal, not mid-cycle
- _The SME segment is far more 'shopping' than the enterprise segment; active evaluation every 1-3 years is common_
